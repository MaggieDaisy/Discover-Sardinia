ABOUT

Little history and facts

Sardinia - a synonym for exclusive holidays, she was able to take care of her tourist image. Elegance and class. The phenomenon of Sardinia can only be understood by getting to know it personally. Sandy beaches, steep rocks, fairy-tale bays, islets, and above all the crystal clear sea with its emerald color, are undoubtedly a breakthrough for the island's values. Sardinia boasts a rich past, the remnants of which are impressive testimonies of the Nuragic civilization. More than 7,000 nuraghes, megalithic structures in the shape of a truncated cone, which still intrigue scientists, have been preserved. Sardinia fascinates with original rituals, folklore, dialect and cuisine based on local products. The most developed tourist destination is the northern part of the island with the famous Costa Smeralda, or the Emerald Coast. The region owes its name to the extremely intense, even mesmerizing color of the sea. A varied shoreline with rock formations, hills descending straight into the sea, Mediterranean vegetation, crystal clear water create unique landscapes. The famous Sardinian beaches are often intimate and always picturesque, hidden in small coves.

Little history and facts

The most famous resort of Costa Smeralda is Porto Cervo - a favorite place of celebrities from around the world, an idyllic town with a huge yacht marina, dominated by the Stella Maris church. In Baja Sardinia on the Gulf of Arzachena, there is the Aquadream water park, and in the vicinity - the famous Ritual disco. South of the Costa Smeralda is the famous resort of Porto Rotondo, named after the port's round shape. The central point of the town is a piazza with boutiques, cafes and restaurants. There are many beaches in the area, including the famous Marinella, known to lovers of water sports. North of the Costa Smeralda lies the popular resort of Palau with wonderful beaches and rocky islets emerging from the crystal sea. The symbol of the city is Orso - a natural rock formation at an altitude of 122 m, which resembles a bear gazing at the sea. From Palau you can go on a cruise around the beautiful islands of the La Maddalena archipelago, with wonderful pristine beaches. While spending your holidays in Sardinia, you should also taste the local wines, which are considered to be one of the best in Italy: for example, the fine white wines of Vermentino di Gallura or strong red Cannonau are a great complement to traditional Sardinian dishes.

What awaits you

Discovering the charming Emerald Coast and its amazing corners. Transfer to the archaeological site "Prisgiona." Visit the Nuraghi Tower, an ancient Bronze Age structure that is a symbol of Nuragic civilization. Then the roads lead through the most famous resorts of the Emerald Coast - Porto Cervo - where international elites from the world of fashion and film traditionally spend their holidays, and Baia Sardinia - the most famous holiday town on the island. During the trip, you can swim in the emerald sea, admire beautiful villas hidden in gardens and watch luxurious yachts in the most famous port of Sardinia, adored by kings, sheiks and rulers from around the world. Fashion lovers will also find some boutiques of renowned companies.

In brief:

  • Orgosolo town with extravagant frescoes
  • Regional Sardinian lunch
  • Visit the museum of Sardinian Traditions

What awaits you

A trip to the heart of Sardinia, the wild and fascinating Barbagia. A visit to a unique town decorated with intriguing frescoes telling about the turbulent history of the island and its inhabitants. The collection of these unique works of art includes over 150 murals with a wide range of styles and techniques. While strolling around Orgosolo, be sure to check out the shop selling beautiful flat caps (bonete in Sardinian), typical headgear for local men from Orgosolo made by a local hatter. A visit to the Museum of Sardinian Traditions in the capital of the region - Nuoro. The tour includes lunch at a restaurant located in the mountains, serving typical Sardinian cuisine.

In brief:

  • Picturesque landscapes of the islands of the La Maddalena archipelago
  • Swimming in the coves of the Emerald Sea

What awaits you

The islands of the La Maddalena archipelago are a National Park consisting of 7 islands and 20 small islets with a very interesting and varied coastline. Start with embarking on a ship in Palau. A trip around the most beautiful small islands of the archipelago: Spargi, Budelli and Santa Maria. Here you can see sandy white beaches, exotic Mediterranean vegetation, rocky shores and, above all, the amazing, intensely emerald color of the sea. Free time in the charming town of La Maddalena, two swimming stops in the beautiful bays in the Park.
